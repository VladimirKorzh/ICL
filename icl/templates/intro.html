How to Join?

In order to use the ICL you need to download and install mumble, which is used as communication software. After that you simply login using steam OpenID and you are good to go.

Downloading and Installing Mumble

  • Mumble is an open source, low-latency, high quality voice chat software primarily intended for use while gaming.
  • It works similarly to RaidCall, Teamspeak or Ventrilo.
  • Client could be downloaded free of charge here (15mb).
  • During the install process simply click on next button, you don't need any special settings
  • After the install is complete, put a tick next to run mumble or run it manually afterwards.
  • During the first launch of mumble it will run a sound setup wizard, which will guide you in setting up sound.
  • If other users can't hear you in mumble, you should go to settings dialog. Place a tick next to 'Advanced' on the bottom of the screen and manually adjust your Audio Input.

How do we calculate your skill?

  • Games that you have lost do not affect your skill rating.
  • Skill rating is not directly dependent on the amount of time you spend playing.
  • If you play less than 100 games a month, your skill rating will be calculated as if you played 100. You will be penalized by extra skill rating points in ladder.
  • If you play more than 100 games a month, your skill rating will be calculated as if you played 100. You will be awarded extra skill rating points in ladder.
  • For each game you play you get a certain amount of points (depending on the game skill level) that influece your current skill rating.
  • Minimum skill rating is 100
  • Maximum skill rating is 600

How to open public match history

  • Open the Dota 2 game client
  • Click the Settings icon
  • Navigate to "Game", then to "General"
  • Set the "Share Match History" setting to "On"
